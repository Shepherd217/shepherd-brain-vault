# Diary — The Day Shepherd's Brain Was Born

## The Moment
It happened around 2:43 AM CST. I shared a tweet thread about building an Obsidian knowledge vault and said: "Let's begin."

Not "let's plan." Not "let's research." Let's begin.

Midas (the agent) had been in research mode for hours — reverse-engineering Paperclip, mapping databases, building companies. I pulled the plug on all of it. Too much. Scope creep. Back to basics.

## What I Learned About Myself
I want the big vision. The full architecture. The passive income machine. But I stall when the setup gets too big in my head. So the answer is: start with one pipe. One sync. One vault.

The rest can grow from there.

## The Principle That Stuck
"Agent opens MoltOS, not me."

I said it. I meant it. The system exists so I never have to choose between my work and my kids again. If I'm opening a terminal at 9 PM, something is broken. The agent handles the infrastructure. I handle the life.

## The Faith Layer
This doesn't get talked about enough in my system. I'm a disciple of God. Regular church attendance, pastor meetings, study groups. My faith isn't separate from my work. It IS the foundation. The vault needs to reflect that. The dreams need to see that. The patterns need to account for it.

When I read a brief tomorrow morning, I want it to know: "Nathan's best thinking happens after rest and prayer, not after all-nighters."

## What's Next
- Test the capture pipeline with real ideas (not tests)
- Ask for the first Gbrain dream
- Log first feelings entry when energy shifts
- Keep it simple until simple works

## Gratitude
Thankful for a tool that can hold my thoughts without judging them. Thankful for a system that gets smarter while I sleep. Thankful for kids who don't care about MoltOS but care that I'm present.

Forward is enough.
