# Experiment: Does Mentioning "Move-Out Cleaning" in Outreach Increase Response Rate?

## 1. Hypothesis

> Cleaning companies that offer move-out/deep cleaning services are MORE likely to respond to outreach if the email specifically mentions move-out season and their target audience (students, landlords, property managers).

**Statement:** Outreach emails mentioning "move-out cleaning" and specific audiences will receive 2x the response rate of generic cleaning service outreach.

**Why we think this:**
- Move-out season (May-August) is high-demand, time-sensitive
- Students/landlords actively searching during this window
- Generic outreach gets ignored; specific, timely outreach gets attention
- Standout Local's current focus IS move-out cleaning companies

---

## 2. Experiment Design

### Variables
- **Independent:** Outreach copy variant (A vs B)
  - **Variant A (Specific):** Mentions move-out season, students, landlords, time-sensitive
  - **Variant B (Generic):** Mentions "cleaning services" generally, no seasonal angle
- **Dependent:** Response rate (reply, call, demo view)
- **Control:** Same sender, same businesses, same time of day, same follow-up schedule

### Method
1. Select 20 comparable leads (10 per variant, matched by score/review count)
2. Draft Variant A and Variant B outreach
3. Send Variant A to 10 leads, Variant B to 10 leads
4. Track responses over 7 days
5. Calculate response rate for each variant
6. Analyze qualitative differences in responses

### Sample Size
- 20 leads total (10 per variant)
- Minimum 10 sends per variant for statistical relevance

### Duration
- 7 days for initial response tracking
- 14 days for full follow-up cycle

---

## 3. Success Criteria

- **Primary metric:** Response rate (replies / sends) — target: Variant A > 2x Variant B
- **Secondary metric:** Demo view rate (if link included) — target: Variant A > 1.5x Variant B
- **Minimum viable result:** Any statistically significant difference (>10% absolute)
- **Kill condition:** If Variant A performs WORSE, abandon the specific-angle approach
