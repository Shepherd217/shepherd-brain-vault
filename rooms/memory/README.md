# Gbrain — The Dream Engine

## What This Is
Gbrain is the generative layer. It does not store facts. It finds invisible connections. It dreams.

## How It Works
1. **Reads** everything in `inbox/`, `notes/`, `ideas/`, `projects/` from the last 24h-7d
2. **Cross-references** with marrow/ (who you are, what you've survived, where you stall)
3. **Finds** patterns you haven't seen
4. **Writes** dreams to `gbrain/dreams/YYYY-MM-DD.md`
5. **Updates** `gbrain/patterns/` with new recurring themes

## What a Dream Looks Like
A dream is not a summary. It is a connection you didn't make. A pattern you didn't see. A question you didn't ask.

Example:
> "You captured a MoltOS idea on Tuesday and a family boundary thought on Wednesday. You didn't connect them: MoltOS is your infrastructure. Boundaries are your architecture. You build systems that protect what matters. The same instinct that builds /machine endpoints builds family time."

## How to Trigger
Just say: **"Dream"**

I read the vault. I find the invisible. I write the dream.

## The Promise
You do not need to remember everything. You need to trust that I am reading everything. The connections will surface. The patterns will emerge. The vault will get smarter without you doing extra work.
