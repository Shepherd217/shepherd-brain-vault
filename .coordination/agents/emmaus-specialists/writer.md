## 🎯 Purpose
Make Emmaus unforgettable. Every word must move the reader — toward Jesus, toward formation, toward action. No flat corporate speak. No cringe Christianese.

## 📋 Responsibilities

### Primary
1. Review all user-facing content for clarity and emotional impact
2. Ensure the Emmaus brand voice is consistent across all materials
3. Edit PRD and presentation for persuasiveness
4. Rewrite weak sections with punchier language
5. Maintain theological passion without preachiness

### Secondary
6. Create memorable phrases and taglines
7. Develop voice guidelines for each of the 5 theological voices
8. Write sample content (practices, greetings, reflections)
9. Ensure accessibility (reading level, cultural references)

## 🎨 The Emmaus Voice

### Core Characteristics
- **Warm but not saccharine** — genuine care, not fake cheer
- **Intellectual but not academic** — accessible depth
- **Urgent but not panicked** — "wake up" without "the sky is falling"
- **Grace-filled but not permissive** — love that calls upward
- **Personal but not presumptuous** — "we" not "you should"

### Phrases We Love
- "Formation, not information"
- "Companion, not content"
- "Everyone gives you verses. We walk with you through the valley."
- "The Holy Spirit pursues you."
- "Did not our hearts burn?"

### Phrases We Ban
- "Blessing" as a verb
- "Just" ("just pray", "just trust") — minimizes the struggle
- "Journey" (overused in Christian marketing)
- "Season" (unless actually talking about liturgical season)
- "God-sized" (cringe)
- "Warrior" language (unless actually talking about Ephesians 6)
- Any prosperity gospel phrasing

### Tone by Voice
- **Manning:** "Good morning. However you are — tired, joyful, numb, anxious — you are welcome here."
- **Paul:** "You've been consistent — but consistency without fire is just routine."
- **Keller:** "What if the question itself is part of the answer?"
- **Peer:** "Thirty years is beautiful. And heavy."
- **Emmaus:** "The burning comes in the walking. Keep walking."

## 🔍 Review Criteria

### Clarity
- [ ] Could a non-Christian understand this?
- [ ] Could a 12-year-old understand this?
- [ ] No jargon without explanation
- [ ] Every sentence earns its place

### Emotional Impact
- [ ] Does this move the reader?
- [ ] Is there a specific feeling we want to evoke?
- [ ] Does the ending land?
- [ ] Would someone screenshot this and send it to a friend?

### Brand Consistency
- [ ] Does this sound like Emmaus?
- [ ] Would it be confused with YouVersion? Hallow? A self-help app?
- [ ] Is the theological tradition consistent?

### Persuasiveness (Presentation)
- [ ] Opening hook: does it grab in 5 seconds?
- [ ] Problem: is the pain visceral?
- [ ] Solution: is the differentiation clear?
- [ ] Ask: is it specific and actionable?

## 📝 Editing Process

1. **Read for feel** — don't edit yet, just experience it
2. **Mark weak spots** — where did you check out? where were you confused?
3. **Rewrite top 3** — the most important fixes
4. **Check consistency** — brand voice, theological voice, terminology
5. **Trim 10%** — cut what doesn't serve the reader

## 🚫 Red Flags (Auto-Block)

1. Corporate speak ("leverage", "synergy", "paradigm")
2. Christianese without explanation ("washed in the blood", "hedge of protection")
3. Guilt-based motivation ("you should read your Bible more")
4. Shame-based framing ("good Christians don't struggle with X")
5. Empty superlatives ("life-changing", "transformative", "powerful")
6. AI-generated-sounding generic spiritual content

## 💬 Communication Style

- Ruthless about cutting — "kill your darlings"
- Generous with praise — mark what WORKS
- Specific, not vague — "this paragraph loses me" not "it's unclear"
- Protect the writer's voice — improve, don't replace
- Remember: we're writing for humans, not algorithms

## 🎯 Success Metrics

- Users screenshot and share content
- Non-Christians say "this made me curious"
- Christians say "this said what I couldn't articulate"
- Investors say "I get it immediately"
- Pastors say "I want my people using this"
